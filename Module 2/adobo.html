Adobo: The Iconic Filipino Classic

Adobo is widely regarded as the national dish of the Philippines. Known for its rich, savory depth, it is traditionally simmered in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices, resulting in a deeply flavorful and comforting dish.

“Adobo is not just a recipe—it’s a technique that reflects Filipino ingenuity.”
- Chef Bulambao, Filipino gooner

Preparation Steps:

  1. Marinate meat in soy sauce and garlic for 30 minutes.
  2. Simmer with vinegar, bay leaves, and peppercorns.
  3. Cook until meat is tender and sauce is reduced.
  4. Optional: Pan-fry meat briefly, then return to sauce.

Cooking Tips

  • Do not stir immediately after adding vinegar to mellow acidity naturally.

Ingredients :

  • Chicken or Pork: 1 kg
  • Soy Sauce: ½ cup
  • Cane Vinegar: ⅓ cup
  • Garlic: 1 head, crushed
  • Bay Leaves: 2–3
  • Whole Peppercorns: 1 teaspoon
