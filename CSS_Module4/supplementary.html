Deep Dive: How a GPU Works

Understanding the silicon. Unlike a Central Processing Unit (CPU), which is designed to handle a few complex tasks sequentially, a Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) is designed for parallel processing. It breaks complex problems into thousands or millions of separate tasks and works on them all at once.

This is why GPUs are efficient at rendering 3D graphics. A single frame in a video game contains millions of pixels. The GPU must calculate the color, lighting, and position of every single pixel simultaneously.

Modern GPUs have evolved beyond just gaming. They are now the engine behind Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning models. The same parallel processing capability used to calculate lighting in Cyberpunk 2077 is used to train Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT.
